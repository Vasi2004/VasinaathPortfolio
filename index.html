In my own words

Optus and Ericsson just achieved a world-first 180MHz 5G standalone carrier aggregation on a live commercial network. I study five minutes from where that team works. That is not a coincidence I am going to waste.

I applied for the Networks stream because it is where my engineering degree and my genuine interests actually meet. Every project I have built has involved designing systems that communicate reliably under constraint — a custom UDP protocol for a four-team distributed network, real-time fault recovery in an embedded controller, REST API integration under failure conditions. Networks is not a stretch. It is where I have already been working.

Optus is also accelerating a 5G rollout through the MOCN agreement with TPG, aiming to hit 2,444 regional sites by 2030. That is active infrastructure growth at a national scale. I want to be part of a team doing real work, not shadowing it. Three months at Optus Networks in the middle of that buildout is exactly where I want to start my career.

Why Networks and not the other streams

Internal Audit and Procurement are legitimate streams with real value. But my strongest projects are all networked systems work. I can contribute to a Networks team from week one without needing to learn an entirely new domain first. In a three-month internship that difference matters. Networks is where I have the deepest foundations and the most to give.
